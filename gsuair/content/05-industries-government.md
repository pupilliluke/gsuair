## WHY AIR QUALITY MATTERS IN GOVERNMENT FACILITIES
Poor indoor air quality within government facilities not only has a direct impact on the health of employees and visitors, but government facilities must also follow strict health and safety regulations enforced by OSHA and the EPA. The higher the occupancy and traffic, the more dust, allergens, and pollutants in the air. Without properly purifying the indoor air, contaminants quickly accumulate.

Along with that, infrastructure and HVAC systems in older government buildings deteriorate over time, which leads to filters not properly circulating clean air. Identified health issues such as Legionnaire's disease, asthma, and hypersensitivity pneumonitis have been linked to poor indoor air quality and/or poor ventilation in such facilities.
